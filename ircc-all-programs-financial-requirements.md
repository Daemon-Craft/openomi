## 1. FEDERAL SKILLED WORKER (FSW) - EXPRESS ENTRY

### Program Code: FSW-EE
### Category: Economic Immigration

### Financial Requirements

#### Minimum Proof of Funds (2025)
**CRITICAL:** These amounts must be available and unencumbered.

| Family Size | Required Amount (CAD) |
|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 person | $15,263 |
| 2 people | $19,001 |
| 3 people | $23,360 |
| 4 people | $28,362 |
| 5 people | $32,168 |
| 6 people | $36,280 |
| 7+ people | $40,392 + $4,112 per additional person |

#### Document Requirements
- **6 months** of consecutive bank statements
- Official bank letterhead required
- All pages must be present
- Account must be in applicant's name or jointly held
- Must show average 6-month balance meeting or exceeding minimum

#### High Priority Red Flags (FSW)
1. **Large Recent Deposits** (within 60 days of application)
   - Any single deposit > $5,000 without source documentation ‚Üí REJECT
   - Multiple deposits totaling > $10,000 ‚Üí REVIEW
   
2. **Borrowed Funds**
   - Any evidence of loans used as proof of funds ‚Üí REJECT
   - Student lines of credit ‚Üí REJECT
   - Credit card cash advances ‚Üí REJECT

3. **Insufficient History**
   - Less than 6 months statements ‚Üí REJECT
   - Gaps in account history ‚Üí REVIEW

#### Acceptable Sources of Funds (FSW)
‚úÖ Employment savings accumulated over time  
‚úÖ Investment accounts (stocks, bonds, mutual funds)  
‚úÖ Family gifts with sworn affidavit (gift deed)  
‚úÖ Sale of property with documentation  
‚úÖ Business income with tax returns  

#### Unacceptable Sources (FSW)
‚ùå Loans from financial institutions  
‚ùå Borrowed money from friends/family (without gift deed)  
‚ùå Funds in locked-in accounts (RRSP without withdrawal proof)  
‚ùå Equity in real estate (must be liquidated)

## 2. CANADIAN EXPERIENCE CLASS (CEC) - EXPRESS ENTRY

### Program Code: CEC-EE
### Category: Economic Immigration

### Financial Requirements

**NO PROOF OF FUNDS REQUIRED** ‚úÖ

**Rationale:** Applicants already working in Canada, assumed to be self-sufficient.

#### Document Requirements
- None for proof of funds
- However, auditors should still review for:
  - Employment income consistency
  - Financial stability (no frequent overdrafts)
  - Evidence of Canadian work experience

#### Red Flags Specific to CEC
‚ö†Ô∏è If applicant voluntarily provides bank statements:
- Check for income consistency with stated Canadian employment
- Verify employment income matches letter of employment
- Flag if income seems insufficient for stated position

## 3. FEDERAL SKILLED TRADES (FST) - EXPRESS ENTRY

### Program Code: FST-EE
### Category: Economic Immigration

### Financial Requirements

**SAME AS FSW** - See Section 1 for amounts.

**Additional Consideration:**
- If applicant has a valid job offer in Canada ‚Üí NO proof of funds required
- If no job offer ‚Üí MUST show proof of funds per FSW table

#### Red Flags Specific to FST
- If claiming "no proof of funds needed" ‚Üí Verify valid job offer exists
- Job offer must be full-time, non-seasonal, in skilled trade (NOC 2021 Major Group 72, 73, 82, 83, 92, 93)

## 4. PROVINCIAL NOMINEE PROGRAM (PNP)

### Program Code: PNP-[Province Code]
### Category: Economic Immigration

### Financial Requirements

**VARIES BY PROVINCE** - See specific requirements below:

#### Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)
**Minimum Settlement Funds:**

| Family Size | Required Amount (CAD) |
|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 person | $15,263 |
| 2 people | $19,001 |
| 3 people | $23,360 |
| 4 people | $28,362 |
| 5 people | $32,168 |
| 6 people | $36,280 |
| 7+ people | $40,392 + $4,112 per additional person |

**Document Requirements:**
- 3 months of bank statements (shorter than FSW!)
- Official bank letter
- Proof of transferability to Canada

#### British Columbia PNP (BC PNP)
**Settlement Funds:**
- Varies by stream (Skilled Worker, International Graduate, Entry Level)
- **General rule:** Show sufficient funds to support family for 3 months
- Typically **lower** than federal requirements

**Document Requirements:**
- 3 months of bank statements
- Can include funds from family members with affidavit

#### Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP)
**Settlement Funds:**
- Must demonstrate ability to support self and family
- **No specific minimum** if employed in Alberta
- If not employed: Show 6 months of expenses (~$15,000 for single person)

#### Other Provinces
- **Saskatchewan:** $10,000 minimum (single), $15,000 (family of 4+)
- **Manitoba:** Lower than federal, ~$10,000-12,000
- **Atlantic Provinces:** Varies, typically lower than federal

#### Red Flags Specific to PNP
‚ö†Ô∏è **Province Mismatch**
- If applying to BC but all funds suddenly transferred from another province ‚Üí REVIEW
- Check for "forum shopping" (applying to easiest province)

## 5. QUEBEC SKILLED WORKER (ARRIMA / QSW)

### Program Code: QSW-ARRIMA
### Category: Economic Immigration (Quebec)

### Financial Requirements

**DIFFERENT FROM FEDERAL!** Quebec sets its own amounts.

#### Minimum Capacity for Financial Self-Sufficiency (2025)

**For 3 months:**

| Family Size | Required Amount (CAD) |
|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 person | $3,462 |
| 2 people | $5,120 |
| 3 people | $6,300 |
| 4 people | $7,650 |
| 5+ people | Add $765 per person |

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCE:** Quebec requires proof for **3 months only**, not 6!

#### Document Requirements
- **3 months** of bank statements (not 6!)
- Must be in French or translated to French
- Official bank attestation (letter)
- Notarized translation if not in French

#### High Priority Red Flags (QSW)
1. **Insufficient for 3-month threshold**
   - Amounts are LOWER than federal, but still strict
   
2. **Translation Issues**
   - English-only documents ‚Üí REVIEW (must be French)
   - Non-certified translations ‚Üí REJECT

3. **Large Recent Deposits**
   - Same rules as FSW but within 30 days (shorter window)

#### Acceptable Sources (QSW)
‚úÖ Same as FSW  
‚úÖ **Additional:** Proof of employment in Quebec (if applicable)  
‚úÖ Quebec family members can provide financial support with affidavit

## 6. FAMILY SPONSORSHIP

### Program Code: FAMILY-SPONSOR
### Category: Family Class

### Financial Requirements

**SPONSOR must demonstrate financial capacity.**

#### Income Requirements (Sponsor)

**For Spouse/Partner/Dependent Children:**
- **NO minimum income requirement** ‚úÖ
- But sponsor must not be receiving social assistance (except disability)

**For Parents/Grandparents:**
- Must meet **Minimum Necessary Income (MNI)** for past 3 years
- MNI is 130% of Low Income Cut-Off (LICO)

**2024 MNI Thresholds (for Parents/Grandparents sponsorship):**

| Family Size | Required Amount (CAD) |
|-------------|----------------------|
| 1 person | $15,263 |
| 2 people | $19,001 |
| 3 people | $23,360 |
| 4 people | $28,362 |
| 5 people | $32,168 |
| 6 people | $36,280 |
| 7+ people | $40,392 + $4,112 per additional person |

#### Document Requirements (Sponsor)
- **3 years** of tax returns (Notice of Assessment)
- Recent pay stubs
- Employment letter
- Bank statements (optional but helpful)

#### Document Requirements (Applicant)
- **NO proof of funds required** from the person being sponsored
- But may need police certificates, medical exams, etc.

#### Red Flags (Family Sponsorship)
‚ö†Ô∏è **Sponsor Issues:**
- Income below MNI (for parents/grandparents) ‚Üí REJECT
- Evidence of social assistance receipt ‚Üí REJECT
- Bankruptcy in past 3 years ‚Üí REVIEW
- Previous default on sponsorship undertaking ‚Üí REJECT

‚ö†Ô∏è **Applicant Issues:**
- If applicant provides bank statements showing large debts ‚Üí NOTE
- Evidence applicant has insufficient funds ‚Üí May impact settlement

## 8. SELF-EMPLOYED PERSONS PROGRAM

### Program Code: SELF-EMPLOYED
### Category: Economic Immigration

### Financial Requirements

**NO SPECIFIC MINIMUM** ‚úÖ

**But must demonstrate:**
- Ability to be self-employed in Canada
- Relevant experience in cultural/athletic activities or farm management
- Financial capacity to support self and family

#### Document Requirements
- 2 years of bank statements
- Proof of self-employment income (tax returns, invoices, contracts)
- Business revenue documentation
- **Show net worth** of at least $100,000 (guideline, not strict)

#### Red Flags (Self-Employed)
‚ö†Ô∏è **Income Inconsistency**
- Stated self-employment income doesn't match bank deposits ‚Üí REVIEW
- No evidence of regular business income ‚Üí REJECT

‚ö†Ô∏è **Insufficient Net Worth**
- Total assets < $50,000 ‚Üí REVIEW
- No clear plan for self-employment in Canada ‚Üí REJECT

## CROSS-PROGRAM FRAUD INDICATORS

### Universal Red Flags (All Programs)

#### HIGH PRIORITY (Automatic Rejection)
1. **Forged Documents**
   - Altered bank statements (fonts, logos, balances)
   - Fake bank letters
   - Photoshopped deposits
   - **Action:** REJECT + Report to IRCC fraud unit

2. **Borrowed Funds Disguised as Savings**
   - Large deposit within 60 days of application
   - Followed by immediate withdrawal after submission
   - Evidence of loan agreements
   - **Action:** REJECT

3. **Money Laundering Patterns**
   - Circular transactions (A‚ÜíB‚ÜíA)
   - Large cash deposits (>$10,000) without source
   - Multiple small deposits to avoid detection ($9,999 repeatedly)
   - **Action:** REJECT + Flag for investigation

#### MEDIUM PRIORITY (Requires Review)
4. **Income Inconsistency**
   - Bank deposits don't match employment letter
   - Self-employed income not supported by tax returns
   - **Action:** REQUEST additional documentation

5. **Financial Instability**
   - Frequent overdrafts (>3 per month)
   - NSF fees regularly
   - Account balance fluctuating wildly
   - **Action:** REVIEW + May request explanation

#### LOW PRIORITY (Note in File)
6. **Minor Issues**
   - Occasional overdraft (1-2 in 6 months)
   - Small unexplained transactions (<$500)
   - Statement pages slightly out of order
   - **Action:** NOTE + May request clarification

## GIFT DEED REQUIREMENTS (All Programs)

### When Funds Are From Family Gift

**Required Documentation:**
1. **Sworn Affidavit (Gift Deed)** including:
   - Donor's full name and relationship to applicant
   - Statement: "This is a gift, not a loan"
   - Amount of gift
   - Date of transfer
   - Notarized signature

2. **Donor's Financial Documents:**
   - Bank statements showing withdrawal
   - Proof of donor's financial capacity
   - May need donor's income proof

3. **Applicant's Bank Statements:**
   - Showing receipt of gift
   - Gift must be in applicant's account for 6 months (FSW) or 3 months (QSW)

**Red Flags for Gifts:**
‚ùå Gift received less than 1 month before application  
‚ùå No gift deed provided  
‚ùå Donor unable to prove financial capacity  
‚ùå Gift amount suspiciously matches exact minimum required

## CURRENCY CONVERSION RULES

**All Programs:**
- Funds in foreign currency must be converted to CAD
- Use **Bank of Canada exchange rate** on date of application
- Provide proof of exchange rate used
- Funds must be **freely transferable** to Canada

**Red Flags:**
‚ö†Ô∏è Funds in restricted countries (capital controls)  
‚ö†Ô∏è Cryptocurrency presented as proof of funds (not liquid enough)

## AUDIT DECISION FRAMEWORK

### APPROVE ‚úÖ
- All requirements met for specific program
- 0-1 low priority red flags
- Funds clearly legitimate and sufficient
- Documentation complete and authentic

### NEEDS REVIEW ‚ö†Ô∏è
- 1 medium priority red flag
- OR 2+ low priority red flags
- Minor documentation gaps
- Inconsistencies that can be explained

### REJECT ‚ùå
- ANY high priority red flag
- Borrowed funds detected
- Forged documents
- Insufficient funds for program
- Multiple medium priority red flags
